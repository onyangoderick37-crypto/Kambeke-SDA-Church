About Our Youth Ministry

The Youth Ministry of Kambeke SDA Church is devoted to helping young people build a strong relationship with Christ, develop leadership skills, and serve their community faithfully. Through the Adventist Youth Society (AYS), our young people participate in worship, outreach, education, recreation, and mission activities every Sabbath and beyond.

AY Meetings: Every Sabbath at 4:00 PM

Our Mission

To lead young people into a saving relationship with Jesus Christ and help them embrace His call to discipleship and service.

Our Vision

To develop a generation of spiritually grounded, morally upright, and socially responsible youth ready to impact the world for Christ.

The Legion of Honor

The Legion of Honor is the Adventist Youth pledge and code of honor that guides all young Adventists in moral, spiritual, and social conduct.

  • Honor for God: Loving and obeying God above all things. (Matthew 22:37)
  • Honor for Parents: Showing respect and obedience. (Ephesians 6:1–2)
  • Honor for Others: Treating everyone with kindness and respect. (Philippians 2:3–4)
  • Honor for Self: Living a pure, healthy life in thought and action. (1 Corinthians 6:19–20)
  • Honor for Duty: Being honest, hardworking, and faithful in responsibilities. (Colossians 3:23)

"Honor is not what others say about you, but what God knows about you."

Becoming a Master Guide

The Master Guide program is the highest level of leadership training offered by the Adventist Youth Ministries Department. It equips youth leaders to mentor, train, and disciple others within the church.

Steps to Become a Master Guide:

  • Be at least 16 years old and a baptized member of the SDA Church.
  • Complete the Pathfinder Leadership Award (PLA) and Adventist Youth Leadership Award (AYLA).
  • Demonstrate active involvement in your local church and community.
  • Complete spiritual, physical, and service requirements as outlined in the Master Guide Manual.
  • Serve faithfully as an example of Christian character and leadership.

Benefits of Becoming a Master Guide:

  • Strong spiritual growth and leadership development.
  • Enhanced skills in youth mentoring, teaching, and community service.
  • Official recognition within the global Adventist Church.
  • Opportunities for mission trips, youth congresses, and spiritual retreats.
  • Fulfillment in serving God and guiding young hearts toward heaven.

Biblical foundation: “Train up a child in the way he should go; even when he is old, he will not depart from it.” — Proverbs 22:6
